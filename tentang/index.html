Tentang

Rabenmutter: a bad mom (“raven mother”). Ukiyo: the floating world - living in the moment, detached from the bothers of life. Ayurnamat: the philosophy that there is no point in worrying about events that cannot be changed. Erkelmek: teasing someone with repeating what they say. Startijenn: a kickstart/boost of energy; also denotes perseverance.. Nutro (Нутро): something that governs your inner being; your core. Buksvåger: literally means “abdomen brothers-in-law”; used for two men who have slept with the same woman. (يقبرني) Ya’aburnee: a declaration of one’s hope that they’ll die before another person because of how difficult it would be to live without them..

Vellichor: the strange wistfulness of used bookshops. Razbliuto: the feeling for someone you used to love but no longer do. Innerer Schweinehund: literal: ‘inner pig dog’; the lazy voice in your head. it symbolizes the part of you that doesn’t want to go jogging, that prefers staying at home with chocolate and pizza and is just generally lazy.. Nunchi: the art of listening to a person and identifying his or her moods or emotions. the literal combination of “eye” and measure”. Shouganai: it cannot be helped.. Weiche: literal: “soft egg”; someone who is weak or cowardly. Ngaobera: a slight inflammation of the throat caused by screaming too much.

Chingada: an imaginary, horrible place where you send all those who annoy you. Wasuremono: forgotten or lost things; an item left behind on a train or forgotten at home.. Mauri: the essence that animates something living, but not human. Zechpreller: a person who leaves a restaurant without paying. Aimonomia: being scared to learn the ‘why’ of something. Muskelkater: charley horse (“muscle cat”).
